Men's Health

Enlarged Prostate (BPH)

Benign Prostatic Hyperplasia (BPH) affects more than half of men over 60, causing symptoms like frequent urination, weak urine stream, and nighttime trips to the bathroom. Dr. Sawkar offers the most advanced minimally invasive solutions available.

Treatment Options

UroLift System

Tiny implants lift and hold prostate tissue away from the urethra. No cutting, no heat, preserves sexual function. In-office procedure. Learn more →

Aquablation Therapy

Robotic waterjet ablation guided by real-time ultrasound imaging. Highly precise tissue removal preserving sexual function across all prostate sizes.

Rezūm Water Vapor Therapy

Convective water vapor energy delivered directly into excess prostate tissue, causing cell death and shrinkage over 3 months.

Explore BPH Treatments

Common BPH Symptoms

  • Frequent urination, especially at night (nocturia)
  • Weak or intermittent urine stream
  • Urgency, straining, or inability to empty bladder fully
  • Dribbling at the end of urination
  • Recurrent urinary tract infections

Good news: You don't have to live with these symptoms. Modern minimally invasive treatments get most patients back to normal within days — without ongoing medications.

Prostate Cancer

Prostate cancer is the most common cancer in men. With early detection and expert surgical care, outcomes are excellent. Dr. Sawkar specializes in robot-assisted radical prostatectomy — the gold standard surgical treatment for localized prostate cancer.

Using the da Vinci Surgical System, Dr. Sawkar removes the prostate with extraordinary precision, carefully preserving the nerve bundles responsible for urinary continence and erectile function. This approach offers superior oncologic control with the best possible functional outcomes.

For patients with more advanced disease, Dr. Sawkar works collaboratively with radiation oncologists and medical oncologists to develop individualized, multidisciplinary treatment plans.

Kidney Cancer

Kidney cancer is increasingly detected incidentally on CT scans ordered for other reasons. Early detection is a significant advantage — many tumors can be treated with kidney-sparing surgery that eliminates the cancer while preserving kidney function.

Dr. Sawkar performs robotic partial nephrectomy — removal of only the tumor and a small surrounding margin, while leaving the rest of the healthy kidney intact. This approach:

  • Preserves maximum kidney function for long-term health
  • Minimizes risk of chronic kidney disease
  • Robotic precision minimizes blood loss
  • Short hospital stay and rapid recovery

Robotic Partial Nephrectomy

Tumor removal while preserving the healthy kidney — the preferred approach for most localized kidney cancers.

Radical Nephrectomy

Complete kidney removal performed laparoscopically for larger or more complex tumors where partial removal is not feasible.

Active Surveillance

For small, slow-growing tumors, careful monitoring may be the most appropriate initial approach, avoiding unnecessary intervention.

Bladder Cancer

Bladder cancer often first presents as blood in the urine (hematuria). Prompt evaluation is essential. Dr. Sawkar offers comprehensive bladder cancer diagnosis and treatment, from initial cystoscopy through definitive surgery.

For non-muscle-invasive bladder cancer, transurethral resection of bladder tumor (TURBT) is the cornerstone of treatment — a minimally invasive endoscopic procedure to remove visible tumors. This is followed by surveillance cystoscopy on a scheduled protocol to monitor for recurrence.

For muscle-invasive or high-grade disease, Dr. Sawkar evaluates patients for radical cystectomy and coordinates multidisciplinary care including oncology input when appropriate.

Warning Signs

  • Blood in urine (hematuria) — painless or painful
  • Frequent or urgent urination without infection
  • Pelvic or flank pain

Any blood in urine should be evaluated promptly — even a single episode. Most cases turn out to be benign, but early evaluation is always the right choice.

Women's & Men's Health

Urinary Incontinence

Urinary incontinence is far more common than most people realize — and far more treatable. Dr. Sawkar provides comprehensive evaluation and individualized treatment for both men and women struggling with bladder control.

Stress Incontinence

Leakage with coughing, sneezing, or exercise. Often treated with minimally invasive sling procedures.

Urgency Incontinence / Overactive Bladder

Sudden, strong urge to urinate. Treated with medications, Botox bladder injections, or nerve stimulation therapy (InterStim).

Post-Prostatectomy Incontinence

Leakage following prostate surgery. Multiple highly effective options including artificial urinary sphincter.

Men's Health

Erectile Dysfunction & Male Sexual Health

Erectile dysfunction affects millions of men and is often a sign of underlying vascular or hormonal issues that deserve attention. Dr. Sawkar provides comprehensive, confidential evaluation and treatment.

Oral Medications

PDE5 inhibitors (sildenafil, tadalafil) are often the first-line treatment and highly effective for many men.

Injection Therapy

Intracavernous injections deliver medication directly, providing reliable erections independent of sexual stimulation.

Inflatable Penile Implant

The gold standard for men who have not responded to other treatments. Highest satisfaction rates of any ED therapy. Concealed, natural-feeling results.

ED as a Health Signal: Erectile dysfunction can be an early warning sign of cardiovascular disease, diabetes, or hormonal imbalance. A comprehensive evaluation may uncover treatable underlying conditions and benefit your overall health.

Male Infertility

Male factor infertility accounts for approximately 40–50% of infertility in couples. A urologic evaluation can identify correctable causes that may allow natural conception.

Dr. Sawkar evaluates men for varicoceles, hormonal imbalances, ejaculatory dysfunction, and obstructive causes of infertility. Many conditions can be corrected surgically or medically, significantly improving fertility outcomes.
