What a kidney stone feels like

A kidney stone is a hard deposit that forms in the kidney and causes trouble when it moves into the urinary tract. The pain can be intense and sudden. The good news is that nearly every stone can be treated, and many never need a procedure at all.

Sudden, severe pain in the back or side
Pain that comes in waves toward the groin
Blood in the urine
Nausea or vomiting
A frequent, urgent need to urinate
Pain or burning when you go

The right treatment for the stone

The best approach depends on the stone's size, location, and makeup. We will confirm which one fits and get you comfortable as quickly as possible.

Small stones · pass naturally

Watchful waiting

Small stones, under about 5 mm, often pass on their own. With extra hydration and medication to ease the way, Dr. Sawkar monitors your progress so nothing is missed.

  • No procedure needed
  • Hydration and medication to help
  • Monitored until it passes
Non-invasive · no incisions

Shockwave lithotripsy

Focused shockwaves break the stone into fragments from outside the body, with no incisions, using sedation for comfort. The pieces then pass naturally over the days and weeks that follow.

  • No incisions or cutting
  • Effective for stones up to about 2 cm
  • Outpatient, home the same day
Any location · laser

Laser ureteroscopy

A slim scope is passed through the urethra and bladder to reach the stone, where a Holmium laser breaks or vaporizes it. No incisions, and highly effective anywhere in the urinary tract.

  • No incisions
  • Works on harder and larger stones
  • Outpatient, home the same day
Long term · stay stone-free

Prevention

Once the stone is handled, the goal is preventing the next one. A metabolic review and a simple, personalized plan can meaningfully lower your odds of forming another.

  • Metabolic and dietary review
  • A plan built around your stone type
  • Fewer recurrences over time
