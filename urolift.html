How It Works

A Mechanical Solution to a Mechanical Problem

Benign Prostatic Hyperplasia (BPH) is simply an enlarged prostate pressing on the urethra ‚Äî like a hand squeezing a garden hose. UroLift uses tiny permanent implants to pull the prostate lobes apart and hold them open, restoring urinary flow immediately.

There's no cutting, no burning, no removal of tissue. The UroLift implants are made of the same biocompatible materials used in other permanent medical implants, and they stay in place indefinitely.

Because the procedure doesn't involve heat or incisions near the ejaculatory structures, it is the only FDA-cleared BPH therapy proven not to cause new, sustained erectile or ejaculatory dysfunction.

The UroLift Procedure

1

Local Anesthesia

Topical numbing is applied. Most patients receive oral sedation ‚Äî no general anesthesia needed.

2

Implant Delivery

A small device is passed through the urethra. Tiny implants are delivered precisely to lift the prostate lobes apart.

3

Immediate Results

The urethra opens as the implants hold the prostate tissue away. Most men experience rapid improvement in flow.

4

The entire procedure typically takes under an hour. Patients are discharged the same day, often without a catheter.

Is UroLift Right for You?

UroLift is FDA-cleared for men with BPH who have a prostate gland between 30 and 80 cc in size. The best way to find out if you're a candidate is a consultation with Dr. Sawkar.

You may be a good candidate if you:

  • Are bothered by BPH symptoms that affect quality of life
  • Want to avoid or stop taking daily BPH medications
  • Want to preserve sexual function
  • Prefer to avoid general anesthesia and hospital stays
  • Have a moderately enlarged prostate (30‚Äì80 cc)

Frequently Asked Questions

Most patients report mild to moderate discomfort during the procedure, which is managed with local anesthesia and optional oral sedation. Post-procedure, some urinary urgency, frequency, and mild burning for a few days are common and resolve quickly.

Most patients do not need a catheter after UroLift. In some cases, a temporary catheter may be placed for a brief period, but this is less common than with traditional surgical approaches for BPH.

Many patients experience noticeable improvement within 2 weeks. Clinical studies show significant improvement in urinary symptoms and flow rates within this timeframe, with continued improvement over the following months.

5-year clinical data shows sustained symptom improvement in the majority of patients. Approximately 13% of patients in clinical trials required additional treatment within 5 years ‚Äî a rate comparable to other BPH therapies.
